Early Blight

Crop: Tomato

Medium

Confidence

Pathogen Type

Fungus (Alternaria solani)

Symptoms & Causes
Organic Treatments
Chemical Remedies

Symptoms:

Concentric rings (target spots) appearing first on older lower leaves. Leaves yellow and prematurely drop.

Primary Causes:

Spores overwintering in debris. Driven by heavy rains, warm temperatures (24-29°C), and wet leaf foliage.

Organic Prevention & Control:

  • Apply organic Copper Fungicide spray early in the season.
  • Mulch plants to prevent fungal spores from splashing up from the soil.
  • Prune lower leaves to maximize airflow and keep leaves dry.

Recommended Pesticides / Fertilizers:

  • Spray Chlorothalonil or Mancozeb on affected plants every 7-10 days if symptoms persist.
  • Ensure balanced nitrogen feeds; excessive nitrogen promotes soft lush leaf growth susceptible to early blight.
